## Summary

Tracking symptoms consistently over time is one of the most valuable things a CIDP patient can do. It documents treatment response, catches "wearing off," identifies flares early, and gives your neurologist objective data instead of recalled impressions. Use this template weekly and bring the pre-visit summary to every appointment.

---

## Main Content

### Why Track Symptoms?

- **Proves treatment is working** (or isn't) — objective data beats memory
- **Documents wearing off** — shows your neurologist that symptoms return before the next infusion, supporting dose/interval adjustment
- **Identifies flares early** — catching a relapse early means faster treatment
- **Supports insurance appeals** — documented functional decline is medical evidence
- **Helps your neurologist fine-tune your treatment** — without data, changes are guesswork

### Quick Daily "Wearing Off" Check

*If you are on IVIG maintenance, track these in the days before your next infusion:*

**Day ___ after infusion** (enter day number each time):

- [ ] Leg weakness returning
- [ ] Foot catching while walking
- [ ] Hand weakness returning
- [ ] Numbness increasing
- [ ] Fatigue worsening significantly
- [ ] Balance more unsteady

If you check 3 or more boxes consistently in the same week before infusion, tell your neurologist. This is evidence that your maintenance interval may be too long or your dose may need adjustment.

### What to Bring to Every Neurology Appointment

**Records:**
- [ ] Printed pre-visit symptom summary (above)
- [ ] Printed question list
- [ ] Infusion/injection log
- [ ] Any new lab results or imaging reports your other doctors sent

**Insurance / access:**
- [ ] Insurance card
- [ ] List of all current medications with doses
- [ ] Notes on any insurance issues or prior auth renewals coming up

**Practical:**
- [ ] A companion who can take notes (recommended)
- [ ] Pen and paper or phone to record answers
- [ ] Note of any new doctors you've seen since last visit

### Describing Symptoms Clearly

Neurologists need specific, observable descriptions. Avoid vague terms — be concrete.

| Instead of... | Say... |
|--------------|--------|
| "My legs feel weak" | "I can't get up from a low chair without using my arms. I couldn't do that 3 months ago." |
| "I feel tired" | "By 2pm I have to stop what I'm doing and rest. That started about 2 weeks ago." |
| "My balance is off" | "I stumbled twice this week on the stairs. I now hold the railing on both sides." |
| "My hands are numb" | "I can't feel the temperature of water with my fingertips. I dropped a glass last week." |
| "I'm having pain" | "Burning pain on the bottom of both feet, 6/10, worst in the evenings, started 3 weeks ago." |

**Template for describing a new symptom:**
> "I have been noticing [symptom] in [location] for about [duration]. It is [constant / comes and goes]. At its worst it is about [X]/10. It [does / does not] affect my ability to [activity]. It started [when — before or after a specific infusion, infection, or event]."

## Key Points

- Weekly symptom tracking is one of the most useful things a CIDP patient can do
- Document consistently so your neurologist can see trends, not just snapshots
- The "wearing off" check before each infusion helps identify under-dosing or too-long intervals
- Print and bring the pre-visit summary to every appointment
- Concrete, specific descriptions of symptoms are far more useful to your neurologist than general statements

## Warnings / Contraindications

None — this is a patient self-reporting tool. If symptoms are severe, worsening rapidly, or causing new safety risks (falling, unable to walk), contact your neurologist promptly rather than waiting for the next scheduled appointment.
