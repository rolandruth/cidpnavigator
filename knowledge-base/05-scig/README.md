# 05 — SCIg (Subcutaneous Immunoglobulin)

## What belongs here

- Mechanism: same immunoglobulin as IVIG, different delivery route
- FDA-approved SCIg products for CIDP: Hizentra (20%), Cuvitru (20%)
- Dosing: weekly or biweekly, lower volume per dose vs IVIG
- How to self-administer (pump, needle sites, rotation)
- Advantages over IVIG: home administration, steadier serum levels, fewer systemic reactions, patient independence
- Transition from IVIG to SCIg: timing, dose conversion
- Side effects: local injection site reactions (redness, swelling, pain) — usually mild
- Who is a good candidate for SCIg
- Facilitated SCIg (fSCIg) — hyaluronidase-assisted, higher volume per site
- Insurance coverage differences vs IVIG (SCIg often covered under pharmacy benefit, not medical)
