## Summary

Multiple Phase 2 and Phase 3 clinical trials are actively enrolling CIDP patients as of 2025. Several trials test entirely new mechanisms of action — complement inhibition, BTK inhibition, and FcRn blocking — that may expand treatment options beyond IVIG, steroids, and plasma exchange. This document lists trials known to be recruiting and provides guidance on how to find and pursue enrollment.

**Important:** Trial status, eligibility, and enrollment change frequently. Always verify current status at ClinicalTrials.gov before pursuing enrollment.

---

## Main Content

### Why Consider a Clinical Trial?

- Access to investigational treatments not yet commercially available
- Free treatment during trial participation (drug and often related monitoring)
- Contribution to research that may help all CIDP patients in the future
- Rigorous monitoring — participants are often seen more frequently than in standard care
- Some trials offer long-term open-label extensions — ongoing access even after the trial

**Tradeoffs to discuss with your neurologist:**
- Randomization risk — you may receive placebo during the blinded period
- Time commitment — frequent clinic visits, travel
- Eligibility requirements — some trials require stopping current treatment during a washout period
- Unknown safety profile for novel drugs

### Active Trials: New Mechanisms

---

#### Riliprubart (SAR445088) — Complement C1s Inhibitor
**Trials:** VITALIZE (NCT06290141) and MOBILIZE (NCT06290128)
**Sponsor:** Sanofi
**Phase:** 3
**Status:** Actively recruiting (launched 2024, recruiting across 28 countries)
**Mechanism:** Riliprubart inhibits C1s, an enzyme in the classical complement pathway — blocking early complement activation that contributes to nerve myelin damage in CIDP. This is a completely different mechanism from IVIG, steroids, and efgartigimod.

**VITALIZE** (NCT06290141):
- Patients on stable IVIG maintenance therapy are randomized to riliprubart vs. continued IVIG
- 160 patients; 24-week double-blind period (Part A) then 24-week open-label (Part B)
- Primary endpoint: ≥1-point improvement on adjusted INCAT score at Week 24
- Eligibility: Adults with CIDP per 2021 EAN/PNS criteria, INCAT score 2–9, currently on IVIG maintenance

**MOBILIZE** (NCT06290128):
- For patients with CIDP who have not responded adequately to standard treatments
- Riliprubart vs. placebo

**Contact/enrollment:** clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06290141 or clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06290128

---

#### Empasiprubart — BTK (Bruton's Tyrosine Kinase) Inhibitor
**Trials:** EMNERGIZE and EMVIGORATE
**Sponsor:** UCB (pharma company)
**Phase:** 3 (EMNERGIZE); Phase 3 (EMVIGORATE)
**Status:** Recruiting / opening 2025

**Mechanism:** Empasiprubart is a Bruton's tyrosine kinase (BTK) inhibitor. BTK is a signaling molecule critical for B cell activation and macrophage-mediated inflammation. By blocking BTK, empasiprubart may reduce both the antibody production and the direct inflammatory nerve damage in CIDP.

**EMNERGIZE:**
- Expected to start enrolling September 2025
- Target enrollment: ~160 patients with CIDP
- Part A (24 weeks): empasiprubart vs. placebo
- Part B (96 weeks): all participants receive empasiprubart
- Oral drug (not injection or infusion)

**EMVIGORATE:**
- Empasiprubart vs. IVIg head-to-head comparison

---

#### IMVT-1402 — FcRn Blocker (next-generation)
**Trial:** AMPLIFI
**Sponsor:** Immunovant Sciences
**NCT:** NCT07032662
**Phase:** 2b
**Status:** Recruiting (started March 2025, primary completion estimated August 2028)

**Mechanism:** IMVT-1402 is a monoclonal antibody that blocks FcRn — the same receptor targeted by Vyvgart Hytrulo (efgartigimod). However, IMVT-1402 is designed to achieve more selective IgG reduction or a different binding profile. It may offer an alternative FcRn-blocking option in the same class as Vyvgart Hytrulo.

### How to Find and Enroll in a Trial

**Step 1: Search ClinicalTrials.gov**
- Go to clinicaltrials.gov
- Search "CIDP" with filter: Status = Recruiting
- Review eligibility criteria for each trial

**Step 2: Ask your neurologist**
- Your neurologist may be a trial site or know of nearby sites
- Ask: "Am I a good candidate for any CIDP trials currently enrolling?"

**Step 3: Contact the GBS/CIDP Foundation**
- The Foundation maintains a clinical trials page: gbs-cidp.org/category/clinical-trials/cidp-clinical-trials/
- Health Navigators can help match you with appropriate trials: 866-224-3301

**Step 4: Review eligibility carefully**
Common CIDP trial exclusion criteria (you may be excluded if you have):
- Diagnosis less than 6 months ago (many trials require established diagnosis)
- Recent plasma exchange (washout period required)
- Recent treatment with rituximab or other biologics
- Significant concurrent autoimmune disease
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Severe organ dysfunction

**Step 5: Understand what participation involves**
Before enrolling, ask:
- How many visits are required and how far must I travel?
- Will I need to stop my current CIDP treatment? For how long?
- What happens if I worsen during the trial?
- Is there an open-label extension after the blinded period?
- Will I know what I received after the trial ends?

### Long-Term Trial: ADHERE Open-Label Extension

Patients who participated in the ADHERE trial of Vyvgart Hytrulo and completed the study were offered enrollment in the ADHERE OLE (open-label extension). This trial:
- Provides ongoing access to efgartigimod (Vyvgart Hytrulo) for trial participants
- Collects long-term safety data
- 99% of ADHERE participants elected to participate — reflecting high patient satisfaction with the drug

## Key Points

- Multiple Phase 3 trials are actively recruiting in 2025: riliprubart (complement inhibitor), empasiprubart (BTK inhibitor), IMVT-1402 (FcRn blocker)
- These new mechanisms could expand options for patients who fail IVIG, steroids, and efgartigimod
- Clinical trial participation provides free treatment but requires time commitment and randomization risk
- Search ClinicalTrials.gov for current status — trial enrollment opens and closes frequently
- The GBS/CIDP Foundation (866-224-3301) can help match patients to relevant trials
- Always discuss trial eligibility with your neurologist before contacting trial sites

## Warnings / Contraindications

- Enrolling in a clinical trial may require stopping current treatment during a washout period — do not stop treatment without coordinating carefully with your neurologist
- Placebo-controlled trials carry the risk of receiving no active treatment during the blinded period; ensure rescue medication protocols are specified before enrolling
- This document lists trials as of May 2025 — always verify current status at ClinicalTrials.gov
