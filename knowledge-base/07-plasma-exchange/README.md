# 07 — Plasma Exchange (Plasmapheresis / PLEX)

## What belongs here

- Mechanism: removes circulating antibodies and inflammatory proteins from blood
- Indications: used when IVIG and steroids are ineffective or contraindicated
- Procedure: what to expect during a session
- Frequency: typically 3–5 sessions over 1–2 weeks for acute treatment
- Maintenance plasma exchange: some patients require ongoing sessions
- Side effects: hypotension, citrate-related tingling, infection risk (central line), clotting
- Central line vs peripheral access
- Efficacy compared to IVIG (short-term benefit similar, but effects may not last as long)
- Who is a candidate: refractory CIDP, IgA deficiency (contraindication to IVIG)
- Hospital vs outpatient setting
- Insurance coverage and access
