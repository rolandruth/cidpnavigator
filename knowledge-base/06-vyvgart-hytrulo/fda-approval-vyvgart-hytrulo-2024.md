## Summary

On June 21, 2024, the FDA approved Vyvgart Hytrulo (efgartigimod alfa and hyaluronidase-qvfc) for the treatment of adults with chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy (CIDP). This is the first and only neonatal Fc receptor (FcRn) blocker approved for CIDP, and the first new mechanism of action approved for CIDP treatment in decades. It is given as a subcutaneous injection, once weekly.

### Mechanism of Action

Vyvgart Hytrulo works differently from IVIG and corticosteroids:

**FcRn (neonatal Fc receptor) blocking:**
- FcRn is a receptor that normally recycles IgG antibodies, extending their lifespan in the bloodstream
- Efgartigimod alfa is a human IgG1 antibody fragment (Fc fragment) that competes with IgG for binding to FcRn
- By occupying FcRn, efgartigimod prevents IgG recycling, leading to accelerated degradation of all circulating IgG antibodies — including the pathogenic autoantibodies causing CIDP
- Result: significant and rapid reduction in total serum IgG levels, which reduces the immune attack on peripheral nerve myelin

**Why this matters:**
- IVIG works by flooding the system with healthy IgG to dilute and neutralize bad antibodies, and by triggering Fc-receptor-mediated signaling
- Efgartigimod works upstream — it blocks the recycling mechanism that keeps all IgG (including the harmful kind) alive in the body
- This is a genuinely different mechanism, which is why it can work in patients who have had inadequate response to IVIG

**Hyaluronidase component:**
- Hyaluronidase (the "-qvfc" component) is an enzyme that temporarily breaks down hyaluronic acid in subcutaneous tissue
- This allows the drug to spread through the subcutaneous space for better absorption
- Without it, the volume of drug needed for systemic effect would be too large to inject subcutaneously

### ADHERE Clinical Trial

The FDA approval was based on the **ADHERE trial** (Antibody-mediated CIDP: Patients' Helpful Evidence).

**Trial design:**
- Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- 322 adult patients with CIDP
- Patients received weekly subcutaneous Vyvgart Hytrulo or placebo during treatment period
- Primary endpoint: time to first CIDP relapse or discontinuation

**Key results:**
- **69% of patients** treated with Vyvgart Hytrulo (221/322) showed evidence of clinical improvement during the treatment period
- **61% reduction in relapse risk** versus placebo (p<0.0001)
- Clinically meaningful improvement on INCAT disability scale
- 99% of trial participants elected to participate in the open-label extension — indicating high tolerability

### Dosing and Administration

**Standard dose:**
- 1008 mg efgartigimod alfa + 11,200 units hyaluronidase
- Supplied in a single-dose vial
- Administered as a **once-weekly subcutaneous injection**
- Injection time: 30–90 seconds
- Administered by a healthcare professional initially; prefilled syringe for self-injection also available (2025 approval)

**Important note on dosing cycle:**
- Treatment is cyclical — patients are monitored for response
- Those who respond continue; non-responders or those who relapse despite treatment may need alternative therapy
- The ADHERE trial used a run-in period followed by a randomized withdrawal design

### Side Effects

**Common (≥10% of patients in trials):**
- **Respiratory tract infection** (upper respiratory infections, common cold)
- **Headache**
- **Urinary tract infection**

**Serious potential effects:**
- **Infection risk**: Vyvgart Hytrulo reduces all IgG levels, including protective antibodies. This may increase susceptibility to infections. Monitor for signs of infection.
- **Allergic reactions / hypersensitivity**: Serious reactions including anaphylaxis reported; have emergency treatment available
- **Hypotension / syncope**: Infusion-related reactions including drop in blood pressure

**Safety note on IgG reduction:**
- Vyvgart Hytrulo will reduce total serum IgG, including IgG needed for infection defense
- Patients should be monitored for signs of infection
- Vaccination status should be reviewed and updated before starting (live vaccines are not recommended during treatment)

### Who May Benefit

Vyvgart Hytrulo may be most relevant for:
- Adults with CIDP who have had inadequate response to IVIG or SCIg
- Patients who prefer subcutaneous administration over IV infusion
- Patients for whom IVIG is contraindicated or not tolerated
- Patients seeking a new mechanism of action

It should be discussed with a neurologist experienced in CIDP management.

### Important Distinction: Vyvgart vs. Vyvgart Hytrulo

| | Vyvgart | Vyvgart Hytrulo |
|--|---------|----------------|
| Route | IV infusion | Subcutaneous injection |
| Approved indication | Generalized myasthenia gravis (gMG) | gMG + **CIDP** |
| Active drug | Efgartigimod alfa | Efgartigimod alfa + hyaluronidase |

The original Vyvgart (IV) was approved for myasthenia gravis. Vyvgart **Hytrulo** (subcutaneous) is the formulation approved for **CIDP**. Do not confuse the two.

- FDA approved June 21, 2024 for CIDP in adults
- First FcRn blocker approved for CIDP — new mechanism distinct from IVIG
