# 06 — Vyvgart Hytrulo (efgartigimod alfa + hyaluronidase-qvfc)

FDA approved for CIDP in adults: **June 2024**

## What belongs here

- FDA approval announcement and indication
- Mechanism: FcRn blocker — reduces pathogenic IgG antibodies
- Different mechanism from IVIG/SCIg (not replacing antibodies, blocking recycling of harmful ones)
- Administration: subcutaneous injection, weekly
- ADHERE trial results (the pivotal trial for CIDP approval)
- Dosing and administration details
- Side effects: injection site reactions, respiratory tract infections, headache
- Who may benefit: patients with IgG-mediated CIDP who have not responded to or cannot tolerate IVIG/SCIg
- Comparison to IVIG and SCIg
- FDA prescribing information / full label
- Argenx (manufacturer) patient support program
- Insurance and access considerations (new drug, high cost expected)

## Priority documents to add first

1. **FDA drug approval page** for Vyvgart Hytrulo (CIDP indication)
2. **Full FDA prescribing information / package insert**
3. **ADHERE trial publication** (New England Journal of Medicine or similar)
4. Argenx patient support/access information

## Important note

Vyvgart (IV form) was approved earlier for generalized myasthenia gravis. Vyvgart **Hytrulo** (subcutaneous) is the CIDP-approved formulation. Do not confuse the two indications.
